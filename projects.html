The Regional Rice Value Chain Project

The regional rice value chain development project is a 34.12 million dollar project jointly funded by the Government of Sierra Leone, the Islamic Development Bank, and BADEA. The overall goal of the program is to contribute to the country's ambition of becoming rice self-sufficient especially since the country is currently spending a huge sum of money on rice importation.

In its National rice self-sufficiency plan, the Government aims to reverse this situation by 2029. Achieving this goal requires supporting smallholder farmers, the country's critical drivers of rice production. The principal objectives of G.H.A.L. are to carry out land preparation activities (land clearing, ploughing, harrowing, leveling, and seed harrowing) of 1000ha of boliland in Bum Chiefdom for the production of rice through an out-growers scheme as spelled out in the project aim and detailed in the tasks and obligation.

Youth At Risk (YAR)

Youth at Risk (YAR) project by FAO which targets vulnerable male and female youths in 5 communities in Tonkolili Districts where the felt need of youths were translated into livelihood activities such as; poultry management, small ruminant rearing, maize cultivation and construction of community centers. The principal objective of GHAL is to carry out land preparation activities of 25ha (ploughing and Harrowing) for the production of maize.

Policy Shift 2.0 (Machine Ring)

Actively pursuing the ministry's dream to increase production and productivity through mechanical cultivation to achieve food self-sufficiency. With the new policy shift 2.0 on mechanization services (Machine Ring), we were given the task in 2021 to put 1,120ha of lowland/boliland areas under cultivation in Karene District for smallholder farmers and youths to contribute towards boosting production and productivity. In 2022, 825ha of new lowland/boliland areas were cultivated in Karene District for smallholder farmers.

Skills Development Fund

Through the skills development fund project under the Ministry of Technical and Higher Education, a project funded by the world bank group. Global Hub Agribusiness Limited is training 75 young people (30% women) on Tractor, power tiller, and harvester operation, Agronomic practices, integrated livestock practices, essential extension services, and primary packaging and processing of rice

Supporting Smallholder Farmers

Global Hub Agribusiness limited will do land preparation and supply smallholder farmers, with everything they need to grow rice and other cash crops and make percentage arrangements on the returns. Most of our smallholder farmers are in the North, North-West, and South-East regions of Sierra Leone -- Karene District, Bonthe District, Kambia District, and Bombali District. We operate in all of them.

With more productive farms and storage facilities, our out-grower farmers can feed their families and sell their percentage to us or their favorite buyer(s). With more income, farmers can invest in their children's education and build businesses in their communities.
