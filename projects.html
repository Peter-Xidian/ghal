The Regional Rice Value Project

The regional rice value chain development project is a 34.12 million dollar project jointly funded by the Government of Sierra Leone, the Islamic Development Bank, and BADEA. The overall goal of the program is to contribute to the country's ambition of becoming rice self-sufficient especially since the country is currently spending a huge sum of money on rice importation.

The Government in its National rice self-sufficiency plan aims to reverse this situation by 2029. Achieving this goal requires supporting smallholder farmers who are the key drivers of rice production within the country.

The principal objectives of GHAL are to carry out land preparation activities (land clearing, ploughing, harrowing, leveling, and seed harrowing) of 1000ha of boliland in Bum Chiefdom for the production of rice through an out-growers scheme as highlighted in the project aim and detailed in the tasks and obligation.
